The Tropic of Calculus I - A Study Guide of Derivatives and Integrals

March 27th 2023 - A brief summary of undergraduate calculus.

In this post we will cover the very basics of undergraduate calculus, starting with derivatives and then moving on to integrals, stating some important results upon the way.

Derivatives

For a function $f : \mathbb{R} \rightarrow \mathbb{R}$, let $y = f(x)$. We define the derivative to be the function: $$f'(x) = \frac{dy}{dx} := \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{f(x+h) - f(x)}{h} $$ provided the limit exists. It is a function on the values of $x$ for which the above limit exists. From this definition we get the following rules:

  • Product Rule: $$[fg]'(x) = f'(x)g(x) + f(x)g'(x)$$
  • Quotient Rule: $$\left[ \frac{f}{g} \right]'(x) = \frac{g(x)f'(x) - f(x)g'(x)}{g(x)^2}$$
  • Chain Rule: $$ [f(g(x))]' = f'(g(x))g'(x)$$
    • Using these rules we can compute the derivative of many common functions, like polynomials, rational functions and compositions of such functions.
